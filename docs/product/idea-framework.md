# Micro-SaaS Idea Generation Framework

A repeatable formula to generate and validate micro-SaaS ideas.

---

## The Formula

### 1. Pick an Audience

Target tradespeople with high hourly rates — they bill $100–200/hr, so any time saved has immediate, quantifiable value.

| Trade | Hourly Rate |
|-------|-------------|
| Plumber | $100–200 |
| Electrician | $100–200 |
| HVAC tech | $100–200 |
| Landscaper | $50–150 |
| Cleaner | $50–150 |
| Lawyer (solo) | $200–500 |
| Accountant (solo) | $100–300 |
| Photographer | $100–250 |
| Tutor | $50–150 |
| Personal trainer | $60–150 |

Key traits:
- Self-employed or small team (no IT department)
- Work is physically draining — they have zero energy for tech
- They want to pay a fee and be done with it

### 2. Find the Time Leak

What do they spend 2+ hours/week on that isn't their craft?

Common time leaks:
- Invoicing and chasing payments
- Scheduling and rescheduling jobs
- Following up on leads and quotes
- Managing reviews and reputation
- Basic bookkeeping and expense tracking
- Client communication (reminders, updates)
- Inventory and parts ordering
- Payroll for small crews

### 3. Price Anchor

`Their hourly rate × hours saved per month = value delivered`

| Hours Saved/Month | Their Hourly | Value to Them | Your Price |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| 5 hrs | $150 | $750 | $25/month |
| 10 hrs | $150 | $1,500 | $49/month |
| 20 hrs | $150 | $3,000 | $79/month |

Rule: your price must be **less than the pain of building in-house**. They don't need to love your product — they need to hate the alternative less.

### 4. Distribution Check

Can you find 50 of them in one city?

Sources:
- Google Maps search by trade + city
- Yelp business listings  
- Local trade associations (e.g., PHCC for plumbers)
- Facebook groups for contractors
- Thumbtack / HomeAdvisor (see who's listed)
- Chamber of Commerce directories
- Craigslist services section

**Rule:** If you can't name 100 businesses in one city to cold-contact, the audience is too niche or too hard to reach.

### 5. Feature Ladder

What 3 features can you add post-launch to deepen the moat?

- Feature 1 — immediate value (solves the time leak)
- Feature 2 — switching cost (stores their data, templates, preferences)
- Feature 3 — moat deepener (something they'd have to rebuild from scratch)

Each feature makes "build it myself" less viable.

---

## Scoring Matrix

Rate each idea 1–5:

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Audience easy to identify | /5 |
| Time leak clear and painful | /5 |
| Pricing is a no-brainer | /5 |
| Distribution channels exist | /5 |
| Feature ladder is obvious | /5 |
| Can launch in 4–5 days | /5 |

**≥ 25 → build it. 20–24 → research more. < 20 → discard.**

---

## The Launch Loop

```
Idea → Validate (talk to 5 customers) → Build (4-5 days) → Launch →
→ Get 50 customers in city 1 → Add features → Move to city 2 → Repeat
```

---

## Moat Reminder

The customer's alternative is **not a competitor**. It is **building it themselves**.

Cost of building in-house:
- Time spent
- Cognitive load
- Learning the domain
- Maintaining and updating
- Fixing bugs
- Ongoing hosting and infra

If your price is less than that pain, you win. Every additional feature widens the gap.
