# Product

Marketing is not a separate phase that happens after building. It must be integrated into product development from day one.

## The Core Principle

When someone goes about their day, they carry their own problems and worries. Marketing is the act of convincing them to buy your product — and the best way to do that is to genuinely solve their problem.

You cannot market a shitty problem. If the problem isn't real, no amount of messaging will save you. That is why it is important to solve **good problems** — real problems that people actually have.

This is part of a software developer's job. It's not just coding.

## Solve the Problem, Minimize Friction

The first thing is: solve the problem. Then present the solution in a manner that has the **least possible friction**.

- The message must be **persuasive** — it should make the user immediately understand why this matters to them.
- The onboarding must be **easy** — as few steps as possible from "heard about it" to "getting value."

## The Aha Moment

The product should have a moment where the user thinks: *"I want to use this."*

That moment is what converts a trial into adoption. Everything before it is just getting out of the way.

## Retention: Grow With the User

For retention, it is critical that your product grows according to the needs of the user. Do not let the user's needs outgrow your product.

If your product stays still while the user advances, they will leave. The product must evolve alongside them — adding depth, capability, and value as their requirements become more sophisticated.
